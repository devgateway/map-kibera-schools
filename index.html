    Kibera, the most populous slum in Nairobi (and East Africa), once appeared as a blank space on most maps because it was considered an illegal settlement.

  • Launch of OpenSchoolsKenya

    The most basic school information -- like school location, fees, class size, or building types -- is unavailable, inaccessible, or out of date. This is especially true in many informal urban settlements, like the Kibera slum in Nairobi, Kenya, where demand for better education data is already well-established. That's where Open Schools Kenya comes in.

    About

    Citizen data – like schools mapped in OpenStreetMap – should be easy to combine and compare with official sources. Information should be accessible and useful to everyone from parents to school directors to government. Feedback on schools will help policy decisions and parent school choices.

    Open Schools Kenya seeks to make data mean better education for all. Learn more.

    Currently, Open Schools Kenya covers all of Kibera and a portion of Mathare (note: Mathare data is in progress and still incomplete). We intend to continue to increase coverage. Please inquire if you wish to partner, contribute data, or have any questions.
